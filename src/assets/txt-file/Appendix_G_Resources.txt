FINAL NOTES FOR INTERVIEW SUCCESS
==================================================================

THE 3 THINGS INTERVIEWERS ARE REALLY EVALUATING:
1. Can you do the job? (technical competence)
2. Will you do the job? (motivation, work ethic)
3. Will you fit with the team? (communication, attitude)

For this specific role — all three matter, but #2 and #3 matter MORE because of the driver experience gap. Show genuine enthusiasm for systems programming. Ask smart questions about their hardware. Demonstrate that you've already started learning (bring up the echo sample or character driver you built).

YOUR BIGGEST COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE:
5 years of C# + architecture + ASP.NET Core expertise is rare in the driver world. Most driver developers don't know how to build scalable APIs, design clean architectures, or build modern .NET applications. You bring complementary skills — the role likely involves more than just driver code. Lean into that.

THE DRIVER GAP:
Be honest but positive. "I've never shipped a production driver" is better than implying you have. Show the work you've done to bridge the gap. Ask how long it typically takes a new hire to get productive on driver work — this shows realistic self-awareness and respect for the domain.

REMEMBER:
The goal of an interview is not to prove you know everything.
It's to demonstrate how you think, communicate, and approach problems.
Be curious. Be honest. Be enthusiastic.

Good luck.
