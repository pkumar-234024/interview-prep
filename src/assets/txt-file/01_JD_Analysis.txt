SECTION 1: JD ANALYSIS
==================================================================

REQUIREMENT-BY-REQUIREMENT BREAKDOWN
--------------------------------------

1. GOOD KNOWLEDGE IN APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - Breadth of experience across the SDLC
   - Ability to design, build, test, and maintain applications
   - Understanding of software quality principles

   Your Fit: STRONG
   You have 5 years of full-stack experience with C#, ASP.NET Core, React.js, SQL Server.
   How to Present: Emphasize complete project ownership, CI/CD involvement, and production deployments.

2. STRONG EXPERIENCE IN C#
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - Deep language internals (not just syntax)
   - Async/await, delegates, generics, LINQ, reflection
   - Performance awareness, memory management

   Your Fit: STRONG
   How to Present: Discuss how you used C# features to solve real problems. Mention async APIs, complex LINQ queries, generic repositories.

3. SOFTWARE DESIGN AND ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLES
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - SOLID, DRY, KISS, YAGNI
   - Design patterns (GoF)
   - Layered/Clean/Hexagonal architecture knowledge

   Your Fit: STRONG (assuming you've applied these)
   How to Present: Walk through architecture decisions you made. Describe why you chose a layered architecture or Repository pattern.

4. OOP CONCEPTS
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - True understanding of encapsulation, inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction
   - Not just definitions — real usage

   Your Fit: STRONG
   How to Present: Give concrete examples from your C# codebase. Explain how you used interfaces vs abstract classes and why.

5. EXPERIENCE IN WINDOWS & LINUX DEVICE DRIVERS
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - Kernel mode programming experience
   - WDM/KMDF/UMDF knowledge (Windows)
   - Linux kernel module writing
   - Driver debugging experience (WinDbg, etc.)

   Your Fit: GAP — You have no driver experience
   How to Bridge:
   - Be honest but show eagerness to learn
   - Highlight that C# involves managed runtime internals — conceptually similar to understanding OS layers
   - Study Sections 5 and 6 of this guide thoroughly
   - Download and explore the Windows Driver Kit (WDK) samples
   - Write a simple Linux character driver as a demo

6. OPEN TO WORK/LEARN C++
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - Willingness to work in unmanaged, pointer-heavy code
   - Understanding of memory management (no GC)
   - Ability to pick up a lower-level language quickly

   Your Fit: MODERATE GAP
   How to Bridge:
   - Study Section 7 of this guide
   - Write small C++ programs using smart pointers, STL
   - Draw parallels to C# — interop, unsafe code, GC vs RAII

7. KNOWLEDGE OF SCRIPTING LANGUAGES SUCH AS PYTHON
   What Interviewer Looks For:
   - Automation, scripting, build tooling
   - Quick data processing or system tasks
   - Not necessarily deep Python — practical proficiency

   Your Fit: SMALL GAP (you likely have some exposure)
   How to Bridge:
   - Study Section 8 of this guide
   - Write a few automation scripts to demonstrate
